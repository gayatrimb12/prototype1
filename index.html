Waterlily: Guided Planning Prototype

This prototype was created as a proposed improvement for Waterlily. The goal is not to change Waterlily’s purpose, but to make its long-term care planning experience easier for older adults and families to understand. Since Waterlily already helps users think about future care needs and costs, this prototype shows how the same idea could be presented in a more guided, step-by-step, and less overwhelming way.

Why this prototype was created

In my paper, I explain that Waterlily is valuable because it helps families prepare for long-term care before a crisis happens. However, one challenge is that care planning can still feel stressful and difficult for some users, especially if they are not comfortable with financial or medical information. This prototype was created to show how Waterlily could improve its current platform by making planning more visual, more guided, and easier to follow step by step.

Main idea: This prototype keeps Waterlily’s long-term care planning purpose, but presents it in a simpler and more supportive format for older adults and their families.

Step-by-step care timeline

Now

Review current support needs, organize medications, and identify who will help with future care decisions.

Next 12 months

Compare home care options, review financial planning strategies, and check whether aging in place will remain realistic.

Longer term

Prepare for possible increases in care needs, explore assisted living options if necessary, and update cost planning over time.

Estimated care cost outlook

Home care estimate $3,200–$4,500 per month depending on support needs
Safety technology estimate $300–$900 for home monitoring and reminder tools
Future assisted care estimate $40,000–$60,000 annually depending on location and care level

Recommended next actions

  • Talk with family about support responsibilities
  • Compare local home care options
  • Review a simple yearly care budget
  • Check in again after six months as needs change
